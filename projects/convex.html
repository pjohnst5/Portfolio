Convex Hull

Highlights

  • Divide and Conquer algorithm
  • O(n*log(n)) complexity instead of O(n^3)
  • Animations to demonstrate algorithm

About

The convex hull of a set Q of points is the smallest convex polygon P for which each point in Q is either on the boundary of P or in its interior. In this project, I developed a program to compute the convex hull of given points using a divide and conquer algorithm as follows:

  1. Divide the set of points into two subsets, L and R.
  2. Computing the convex hulls of L and R recursively where the base case is two points.
  3. Combining the convex hulls of L and R by finding the upper and lower common tangents and making those tangents part of the new combined convex hull.

There were many cases which I had to account for when finding the common tangents, i.e. when the top point of L was lower than the top point of R and vice versa. In addition to this I had to make sure that the search for common tangents stopped at the correct points.
